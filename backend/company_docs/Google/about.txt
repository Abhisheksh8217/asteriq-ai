Google Company Profile and Interview Guidelines
===============================================

Core Cultural Values & Expectation ("Googleyness"):
--------------------------------------------------
1. Doing the right thing: Google employees (Googlers) are expected to act with high integrity, respect their coworkers, and prioritize the user's benefit above all.
2. Intellectual humility and adaptability: The capacity to learn, grow, and adjust to changing requirements rather than holding rigid beliefs.
3. High agency and bias for action: Google values engineers who proactively tackle ambiguous problems, take ownership, and lead without needing explicit authorization.
4. Collaborative spirit: Designing systems together, conducting peer reviews, and mentoring others.

Engineering Excellence Standards:
---------------------------------
1. Simplicity over cleverness: Code should be clean, readable, well-tested, and easy to maintain. Over-engineering is discouraged.
2. System design and scalability: Google services serve billions of queries. Systems must be designed for high availability, low latency, fault tolerance, and proper partitioning.
3. Testing rigour: Write comprehensive unit, integration, and end-to-end tests. Code changes should not proceed without adequate coverage.
4. Monitoring and observability: Software must expose proper telemetry (metrics, logging, tracing) to ensure issues can be diagnosed in production.

Technology Stack:
-----------------
* Languages: Python, Go, C++, Java, Kotlin, TypeScript.
* Infrastructure: Kubernetes, Borg, Protobuf, gRPC, Spanner, Bigtable, TensorFlow.
* Practices: Monorepo workflow, strict style guides, continuous deployment.

Interview Focus Areas:
----------------------
* Algorithm and Data Structures: Space and time complexity (Big-O notation), dynamic programming, graph traversals, and sorting.
* System Design: Scalability, load balancing, caching, database indexing, and network protocols.
* Behavioral: Demonstrating leadership in past projects, navigating teamwork conflicts, and handling failure.
