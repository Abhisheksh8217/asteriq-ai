Amazon Company Profile and Interview Guidelines
===============================================

Leadership Principles (LP) Overview:
------------------------------------
Every Amazon interview candidate is evaluated against the 16 Leadership Principles. The most critical for engineering roles include:
1. Customer Obsession: Leaders start with the customer and work backwards. They work vigorously to earn and keep customer trust.
2. Ownership: Leaders are owners. They think long-term and don't sacrifice long-term value for short-term results. They never say "that's not my job".
3. Bias for Action: Speed matters in business. Many decisions and actions are reversible and do not need extensive study. We value calculated risk-taking.
4. Dive Deep: Leaders operate at all levels, stay connected to the details, audit frequently, and are skeptical when metrics and anecdote differ. No task is beneath them.
5. Deliver Results: Leaders focus on the key inputs for their business and deliver them with the right quality and in a timely fashion.
6. Hire and Develop the Best: Leaders raise the performance bar with every hire and promotion.

Technical and Architectural Standards:
-------------------------------------
1. Decentralized microservices: Amazon popularized service-oriented architecture (SOA) and two-pizza teams. Each microservice must own its data and expose strict APIs.
2. Operational Excellence: Engineers are on-call for the services they write. Reliability, scaling under sudden loads (Prime Day), and fast deployment rollbacks are vital.
3. Minimal operational footprint: Preference for serverless (AWS Lambda) and managed services (DynamoDB, RDS, SQS, ECS) to minimize administration tasks.

Technology Stack:
-----------------
* Languages: Java, Python, C++, TypeScript, Rust.
* Cloud: Extensive usage of Amazon Web Services (AWS) solutions (S3, DynamoDB, Lambda, ECS, Fargate, IAM).
* Practices: Continuous deployment pipelines, automated rollouts, code reviews.

Interview Focus Areas:
----------------------
* Behavioral STAR questions: Situation, Task, Action, Result. Every answer must reference specific numbers and outcomes matching LPs.
* System Design: Focusing on AWS scaling paradigms, service isolation, event-driven architectures, and database choices (NoSQL vs relational).
* Software Engineering: Data structures, object-oriented design patterns, coding with focus on security and efficiency.
